Abstract

In this paper, we propose a novel framework, ExS2D (Extending Single-Arm Agent Actions to Dual Arm), which leverages extensive single-arm datasets to facilitate dual-arm operations without the need for additional dual-arm data. ExS2D introduces an attention-extension mechanism that embeds semantic information into pixel-level data, allowing for the dynamic generation of action pairs. These pairs are processed by the proposed Target Allocation Network (TAN), which allocates tasks between arms, assesses feasibility, and selects the optimal solution.

Furthermore, ExS2D can seamlessly transition to single-arm operation when abnormalities are detected, ensuring uninterrupted task execution. Experimental results demonstrate that ExS2D enhances task efficiency while eliminating the cost of specialized dual-arm data collection.

Framework of ExS2D


ExS2D framework has two main components: attention extension for target generation and target allocation network. Attention-extension identifies key regions, assigns probabilities, and creates masks for efficient pick-and-place tasks. The Task allocation network dynamically allocates the actions generated by the attention extension to the dual arms. ExS2D leverages a motion planning library for effective coordination. If an abnormal state, ExS2D switches to single-arm operation and dynamically adjusts the task plan to ensure successful completion.

Experiments


In our experiments, ExS2D (our method) was compared with three baseline methods across various language-conditioned manipulation tasks, with stage number, stage efficiency, and task completion time recorded. The results demonstrate that ExS2D consistently achieves the highest task completion efficiency with fewer stages, reduced task duration, and superior stage efficiency. Conclusion and Limitations

In this paper, we presented ExS2D, a novel framework that extends the capabilities of single-arm agents to dual-arm manipulation, addressing the inherent limitations of single-arm agents in handling complex dual-arm tasks. By integrating an attention-extension mechanism and TAN, ExS2D dynamically generates and allocates action pairs across both robotic arms, significantly enhancing task efficiency and adaptability. Our experimental results demonstrate that ExS2D achieves superior performance compared to existing methods by reducing the number of stages required for task completion and increasing stage efficiency without requiring retraining for dual-arm operations. Overall, ExS2D represents a promising approach for expanding the flexibility of robotic manipulation systems by leveraging existing single-arm agents while introducing new capabilities for dual-arm coordination. Future work will explore the extension of this approach to multi-arm systems, where the coordination between three or more robotic arms can further enhance task execution speed, efficiency, and robustness. Additionally, while ExS2D performs well in general manipulation tasks, more complex tasks, such as twisting bottle caps or handling flexible objects, remain beyond its current capabilities and will be explored in future improvements
